# Retailers

A retailer in the Elastic application is referred to as a customer, representing the primary user base of the platform. These are business owners who operate physical or online retail stores and need to source inventory from wholesale suppliers.

## Types of Retailers

### Specialty Outdoor Retailers
- **Independent stores**: Like Wilderness Exchange or The Outdoorsman Attic
- **Focus areas**: Outdoor gear, camping equipment, hiking accessories, adventure sports
- **Typical order patterns**: Seasonal buying, pre-season inventory buildup, smaller frequent restocks
- **Pain points**: Managing seasonal demand fluctuations, staying current with latest gear trends

### Multi-Category Sports Retailers
- **Chain stores and franchises**: Larger operations with multiple locations
- **Product range**: Athletic wear, sports equipment, outdoor gear, fitness accessories
- **Ordering behavior**: Bulk purchases, planned seasonal campaigns, promotional tie-ins
- **Challenges**: Coordinating inventory across multiple locations, managing supplier relationships

### Boutique Adventure Stores
- **Niche specialists**: Rock climbing, trail running, backcountry skiing
- **Customer base**: Enthusiasts seeking specialized, high-quality equipment
- **Purchasing style**: Quality over quantity, early adoption of innovative products
- **Requirements**: Detailed product information, technical specifications, expert-level support

## Retailer Needs and Motivations

### Primary Goals
Retailers want to purchase inventory with the least friction possible - anything that streamlines the process and gets them back to serving customers is valuable.

### Speed and Efficiency
- **Quick reorders**: Ability to rapidly replenish popular items
- **Bulk operations**: Add multiple items to cart simultaneously
- **Saved preferences**: Remember frequent orders and preferred suppliers
- **Mobile accessibility**: Place orders while on sales floor or at trade shows

### Business Intelligence
- **Market trends**: Understanding which products are gaining popularity
- **Seasonal timing**: When to place orders for optimal inventory turnover
- **Profit margins**: Clear visibility into wholesale vs. suggested retail pricing
- **Inventory planning**: Tools to forecast demand and avoid overstock/stockouts

### Relationship Management
- **Brand partnerships**: Building strong relationships with key suppliers like Salomon, New Balance, or Patagonia
- **Exclusive access**: Early access to new product launches or limited editions
- **Support resources**: Marketing materials, product training, sales support
- **Payment flexibility**: Net terms, volume discounts, promotional pricing

## Seasonal Considerations

### Spring/Summer Focus
- **Peak categories**: Hiking boots, camping gear, water sports equipment
- **Ordering timeline**: January-March for spring inventory
- **Challenges**: Predicting weather-dependent demand, managing storage space

### Fall/Winter Preparation
- **Key products**: Winter jackets, snow sports equipment, cold weather accessories  
- **Critical timing**: July-September orders for holiday season
- **Considerations**: Higher price points, longer selling cycles, weather variability

## Success Factors

### Efficient Retailers Typically
- **Plan ahead**: Use historical data and trend analysis for purchasing decisions
- **Diversify suppliers**: Don't rely on single brands to reduce supply chain risk
- **Monitor margins**: Balance popular brands with higher-margin specialty items
- **Engage customers**: Use product knowledge to drive sales and build loyalty

### Common Pain Points
- **Minimum order quantities**: Balancing MOQs with inventory investment
- **Shipping logistics**: Coordinating deliveries to minimize disruption
- **Returns processing**: Managing defective or slow-moving inventory
- **Competitive pressure**: Staying competitive with online retailers and big box stores
